## Summary of Findings

A single `whoami` command was executed on the agent named **dc** — strongly indicating the domain controller — by the **CONDEF\Administrator** account at 00:46 UTC on 2026-06-29. The parent process was `cmd.exe`, suggesting interactive or scripted command-line activity. While `whoami` is a benign built-in, its execution on a domain controller under the domain Administrator account at an off-hours timestamp is a significant discovery indicator. Adversaries routinely run `whoami` immediately after establishing a foothold or escalating privileges to confirm their effective identity before pivoting further.
